# What are the CFD Pending Orders?

Pending Orders allow you to set a specific price at which you want to enter or exit a trade rather than executing it at the current market price. Once the instrument's price reaches your target, the platform automatically executes the order, making it a useful tool for trading without constantly monitoring the market.

How do pending orders work?

The system executes a Pending Order as soon as the price of the instrument reaches the set level. These orders can be used in two ways:

*   **Entering a Position:** Open a trade at a predefined price.
*   **Closing a Position:** Exit an existing trade at a specific price.

What are the types of pending orders?

*   [**Limit/Stop orders:**](https://helpcentre.trading212.com/hc/en-us/articles/360007435938-What-are-Limit-Stop-entry-orders-on-CFD)These orders allow you to open a new position once the instrument's price reaches your preset target.
*   **Risk-limiting orders****:** Orders such as [Take Profit & Stop Loss](https://helpcentre.trading212.com/hc/en-us/articles/360007435978) and [Trailing Stop Loss](https://helpcentre.trading212.com/hc/en-us/articles/360008658418) are linked to an existing position and automatically close the trade when the specified price is reached.

Why was a pending order with TP/SL cancelled even after the entry price was hit?

Standard limit/stop entry orders can have attached Take Profit (TP) and Stop Loss (SL). If the first tradable price (e.g., at the open or after a gap) is at or beyond the TP or SL, those exits are no longer valid for the intended entry. To prevent ambiguous execution, the entire order (entry + TP/SL) is cancelled.

💡 **Example**

Buy Limit at €100 with TP €101 and SL €98 → market opens at €102 or €97 → order cancelled
